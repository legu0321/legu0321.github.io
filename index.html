Mission

Use NLP for early Alzheimer's detection

Incidence of neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimerâ€™s and Dementia is on the rise in elderly populations. Early medical diagnosis can be expensive, invasive and time-consuming. Prior research suggests that linguistic capability decline can occur many years and even decades before primary symptoms.

Blessed by the fast growing NLP technologies and Machine Learning algorithms, we aim to create a fully automated, easy-to-use and relatively accurate tool that early identify language decline patterns over the time and signs of potential development of Alzheimer's and Dementia.

Methodology

Linguistic Features Computation

Based on dozens of researches and academic papers, we computed more than 300 linguistic features, that could potentially indicates spoken language capabilities. For example, Vocabulary Richness, Repetition, and Use of Passive etc.

Difference in Difference Analysis

We used the statistical technique of Difference in Differences (DID or DD), to assess the effect of Alzheimer's on linguistic features by comparing the average change over time for the patient group, compared to the average change over time for the control group.
Regression on outcome = β0 + β1 × A + β2 × T + β3 × A × T

Analysis on β3

We picked the significant linguistic features with the most effect size based on β3>, and use them as major features for comparison for our "Text Analyzer".
